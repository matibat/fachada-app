## Overview

Chromatic Drift is a generative art series that uses prime numbers as seeds to create large-format colour field paintings. The algorithm draws inspiration from Mark Rothko and Ellsworth Kelly — fields of colour that bleed, layer, and resolve.

## Process

Each piece runs in two passes: a low-frequency structural pass that lays the compositional skeleton, and a high-frequency detail pass that adds texture, grain, and edge dissolution. The result is printed at 120×90cm on archival cotton paper.

## Exhibition

Shown at the Digital Forms gallery in Tokyo, March 2025. Three pieces acquired by private collectors.
